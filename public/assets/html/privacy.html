2. Purposes & Legal Bases

We process data submitted via the contact form for the following purposes:

  • Responding to your inquiry and managing follow-up communication;
  • Preventing misuse and ensuring service security (e.g., spam/captcha, rate-limiting);
  • Documenting communication to establish, exercise, or defend legal claims where necessary.

Legal bases under Art. 6 GDPR:

  • Art. 6(1)(b) GDPR – performance of a contract or steps prior to entering into a contract (if your inquiry relates to a contract or service request);
  • Art. 6(1)(a) GDPR – consent (e.g., optional callback or marketing consent, where expressly given);
  • Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR – legitimate interests (efficient handling of inquiries, service integrity and IT security).

3. Data Categories Collected via the Contact Form

  • Identification: name (first/last);
  • Contact: email address, phone (optional);
  • Message content: subject, free-text message;
  • Security data: simple arithmetic challenge answer (captcha);
  • Technical metadata: date/time, HTTP headers (incl. IP address), user agent (logged server-side for security and troubleshooting).

We do not use third-party CAPTCHA services on the contact form; a local challenge is used instead.

4. Recipients & Processing

Your message is delivered to us by email. For this we use our email provider and hosting services:

  • Hosting/Infrastructure: [[Hosting provider (e.g., Hetzner Online GmbH)]] – Germany/EU;
  • Email transmission (SMTP): [[Provider, e.g., Hetzner]] – Germany/EU.

These service providers act as (sub-)processors under Art. 28 GDPR on the basis of appropriate data processing agreements. Within our organisation, access is limited to persons who need the data to process your inquiry (need-to-know principle).

5. Third-Country Transfers

We do not intentionally transfer contact form data to countries outside the EU/EEA. If an exceptional transfer is necessary (e.g., you request contact via a non-EU channel), we will ensure appropriate safeguards under Art. 44 ff. GDPR.

6. Storage Periods

  • Message content: generally retained for 12 months to handle your request and document communication;
  • System/security logs: typically retained for 12 weeks (short-term) unless needed for incident analysis;
  • If legal retention obligations apply (e.g., commercial or tax law), we retain data as required by law and restrict processing accordingly.

7. Your Rights (GDPR)

You have the right to request access (Art. 15), rectification (Art. 16), erasure (Art. 17), restriction (Art. 18), and data portability (Art. 20), and to object to processing based on Art. 6(1)(f) (Art. 21). Where processing is based on consent, you may withdraw consent at any time (see Section 8).

8. Consent & Withdrawal

If we rely on your consent (Art. 6(1)(a) GDPR) for optional features, you may withdraw that consent at any time with effect for the future by contacting us. The lawfulness of processing prior to withdrawal remains unaffected.

9. Obligation to Provide Data

You are not legally obliged to provide personal data. However, without essential information (e.g., your email address and a meaningful message), we cannot process your inquiry. Optional fields are marked accordingly.

10. Security

We implement appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect your data (Art. 32 GDPR), including TLS encryption during transmission, server hardening, least-privilege access, and audit logging. The contact form uses a local arithmetic challenge to mitigate automated abuse.

11. Complaints

You have the right to lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority, in particular in the EU Member State of your habitual residence, place of work, or place of the alleged infringement. In Germany, this is typically your federal state’s data protection authority.

12. Changes to This Notice

We may update this notice to reflect legal or technical changes. The “Last updated” date at the top indicates the current version.
